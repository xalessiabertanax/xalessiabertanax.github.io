Case Study

VR Exposure Therapy

A safe virtual environment for train conductors to process trauma through gradual exposure

Role: VR Developer & Designer
Year: 2023
Duration: 3 months
UnityC#Level DesignVR Development
View Figma File →

01

The Context

Train conductors often face traumatic incidents on the railway, ranging from aggression to fatal accidents. Recovering from these events is difficult, and returning to the physical location of the trauma can be overwhelming.

Key Pain Points

1Conductors struggle to return to the site of traumatic incidents
2Traditional therapy lacks the ability to recreate controlled environments
3Retraumatization risk is high without careful pacing and control
4Need for a gradual, therapist-controlled exposure tool

02

The Process

01

Research & Scoping

Studied gradual exposure therapy principles and worked with the research team to define psychological safety requirements.

02

Level Design

Designed the VR environment in Unity with focus on psychological safety, realistic enough to trigger memory processing, not so hyper-realistic it caused retraumatization.

03

Safety Features

Implemented a 'safety anchor', a virtual object the user can look at to instantly dim the environment, and adjustable crowd density for the therapist.

04

Sensory Design

Lighting and sound design avoided harsh, sudden noises. Focused on ambient station sounds to ground the user in the experience gently.

03

The Solution

A VR Gradual Exposure Therapy tool that recreates the train station environment in a completely safe, therapist-controlled manner.

✓Gradual exposure VR environment built in Unity
✓Safety anchor for instant environment dimming
✓Adjustable crowd density controlled by therapist
✓Ambient sound design for gentle grounding
✓Multiple exposure levels with progressive intensity

04

The Impact

The VR tool provides a controlled, safe environment for conductors to process trauma without the risks of real-world exposure.

Key Learnings
