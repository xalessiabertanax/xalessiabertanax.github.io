Case Study

Affective Computing Research

Exploring how machines can recognise and respond to human emotion through physiological signals

Role: Researcher
Year: 2023
Duration: 3 months
PythonEEGLiterature ReviewSignal Processing

01

The Research Question

Can physiological signals (EEG, skin conductance, heart rate) be reliably used to detect and classify emotional states in real-time — and what are the ethical implications?

Key Pain Points

1Emotion recognition from physiological data is inherently noisy and subjective
2Cultural and individual differences affect emotional expression
3Ethical concerns around consent, privacy, and manipulation
4Gap between lab-controlled studies and real-world applicability

02

The Process

01

Literature Review

Surveyed state-of-the-art research in affective computing, focusing on EEG-based emotion recognition and multimodal signal fusion.

02

Signal Analysis

Explored methods for extracting emotional features from EEG signals, including frequency band analysis and temporal patterns.

03

Ethical Framework

Developed an ethical analysis of affective computing applications, addressing consent, bias, and potential misuse.

04

Future Directions

Proposed recommendations for responsible development of emotion-aware systems in HCI contexts.

03

The Contribution

A comprehensive research paper examining the state of affective computing, with practical insights for HCI practitioners designing emotion-aware systems.

✓Survey of EEG-based emotion recognition methods
✓Analysis of multimodal signal fusion approaches
✓Ethical framework for affective computing in HCI
✓Practical guidelines for responsible emotion-aware design

04

Key Findings

The research highlighted both the promise and the limitations of current affective computing approaches, emphasizing the need for ethical guardrails.
