Case Study

Norlandia, Design Thinking

Applying design thinking to reimagine childcare service experiences

Role: Design Thinking Facilitator
Year: 2024
Duration: 3 months
FigJamMiroFigmaDesign Thinking
View Figma File →

01

The Challenge

Norlandia needed to better understand the needs and pain points within their childcare services to improve both parent and staff experiences.

Key Pain Points

1Limited insight into day-to-day pain points experienced by parents and staff
2Service touchpoints were inconsistent across different locations
3No structured process for gathering and acting on feedback
4Need for a human-centered approach to service improvement

02

The Process

01

Empathize

Conducted interviews and observations with parents, staff, and management to understand the full service ecosystem and emotional journey.

02

Define

Synthesized research findings into clear problem statements, personas, and journey maps highlighting key friction points.

03

Ideate

Facilitated brainstorming workshops with cross-functional stakeholders to generate solutions addressing root causes.

04

Prototype & Test

Created service blueprints and low-fidelity prototypes of proposed improvements, validating concepts with actual users.

03

The Solution

A comprehensive service design proposal with actionable improvements across key touchpoints in the childcare experience.

✓Service blueprints mapping the entire parent and staff journey
✓Identified quick wins and long-term strategic improvements
✓Stakeholder-aligned roadmap for implementation
✓Design thinking toolkit for ongoing service innovation

04

The Impact

The project delivered actionable insights and a structured framework for Norlandia to continuously improve their childcare services.

Key Learnings
